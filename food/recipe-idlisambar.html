Idli Sambar

A South Indian classic combination of fluffy idlis with flavorful sambar and coconut chutney!

ü•î Ingredients for Idli:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Water (for grinding)
  • Salt to taste

ü•£ Ingredients for Sambar:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp sambar powder
  • 1/2 tsp tamarind paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh curry leaves
  • 1 tbsp oil

üë®‚Äçüç≥ Instructions:

  1. Wash rice and urad dal separately. Soak both for 6-8 hours.
  2. Grind the rice and dal mixture with fenugreek seeds and water into a smooth batter.
  3. Ferment the batter for 8-12 hours, depending on the weather.
  4. For sambar, cook toor dal in a pressure cooker with water until soft.
  5. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, and let them splutter. Add onions, tomatoes, turmeric, and red chili powder.
  6. Add cooked dal, tamarind paste, sambar powder, and water to make a thick soup-like consistency. Cook for 10 minutes.
  7. Steam the idlis in a steamer for 10-12 minutes, or until they are soft and fluffy.
  8. Serve the idlis hot with sambar and coconut chutney on the side.
